Clyde Machine is Joseph Greene, bass music producer operating out of Seattle, WA. He plays dubstep, drum & bass, and trap music. His live performance is a wild and energetic act, and fits the setting for nightclubs and venues frequented by electronic dance music acts. He has provided opening support for HYPERCRUSH and Split Breed, as well as sharing the stage with various local music acts.
Clyde Machine has released numerous EPs and singles since beginning his production career in 2012, with works signed to Better Than Records, DropZone Records, and Piko Piko Detroit. Joseph writes and releases original music, with occasional remixes and licensed work for indie game developers and video-makers.

  • YourEDM Premiere: Clyde Machine - Valkyrie Light [Better Than Records] - Excerpt from Matt Meadow, February 22, 2015
    • “Valkyrie Light” is the first track on the EP, a stellar post-apocalyptic dubstep tune. The soaring lead synths give off a feeling of impending doom and terror, while the modulated bassline and crisp drums are reminiscent of a future sci-fi thriller. The backing vocals, chopped up and spliced throughout the tune, help to add to the sense of futurism and suspense before the drops. The piano, as well, in the outro is a welcome and magnificent touch – a great way to put a “period” at the end of an otherwise great tune.
  • MDN: Clyde Machine Releases New EP Today - Excerpt from Midland Daily News, March 2, 2015
    • The “Correct Me If I’m Wrong EP” marks Greene’s first label signing. The EP features five tracks of electronic dance music in various moods and styles. The songs featured on this release range from hard-hitting music to laid back relaxed sounds. The music incorporates various live-recorded samples with synthesized instruments. Listeners familiar with names such as Kill The Noise, Skrillex, What So Not and Dillon Francis will find Clyde Machine music within their taste.
  • YourEDM: Songs For A Rainy Day - Excerpt from Matt Meadow, February 28, 2014
    • I can’t even really begin to describe the feelings that I get when I listen to it – Clyde Machine has translated emotion into music so acutely that it astounds me. For one, his production has soared in quality. The depth of the bass and the timbre of the drums, how real they sound, makes everything about this EP sparkle. These are tracks that you would listen to when you want to make something beautiful. Second, there is real life embedded in the very heart of this release. It is the direct result of an event in the producer’s life – not a run-of-the-mill “club banger.”
  • Magnetic Mag: TOP 10 DRUM & BASS SONGS OF THE WEEK - Excerpt from Matt Meadow, February 15, 2014
